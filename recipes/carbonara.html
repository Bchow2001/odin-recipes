Carbonara Recipe

Description

Carbonara is an Italian dish originating from Rome. It is made with eggs, hard cheese, cured pork, and lots of black pepper. Timing is everything with this dish, so practice is very welcome.

I had this meal for the very first time in Rome with my very bad friend

Daphne Elena Pezij

It is one of my favourite dishes to make, being both flavourful, classy and most importantly of all easy to make.

Ingredients

  • 4 oz. Guanciale (soft cured pork jowl), Pancetta is an acceptable substitute, bacon is not
  • 2 oz.Pecorino Romano or Parmigiano Reggiano (similar mild hard cheeses are acceptable)
  • 4 Eggs, use an extra egg yolk for richness
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • Olive oil
  • Spaghettoni or Spaghetti

Steps

  1. Boil salted water
  2. While water is boiling start frying the pancetta
  3. Crack eggs into a bowl, beat, then add grated cheese and lots of pepper
  4. Mix eggs, cheese and pepper together
  5. Add pasta to boiling water
  6. Fry pancetta until crispy then turn off heat
  7. Once pasta is finished cooking, add pasta to the pan and mix well
    • You are making a fat sauce with the water from the pasta and the rendered fat from the pancetta
  8. Gently pour egg mix into pan once the pan has cooled off enough
    • The pan should basically be at the temperature of the pasta
    • It is important that you do not cook the eggs, otherwise you are simply making scrambled eggs
  9. Serve with a generous amount of grated cheese and pepper on top
    • I am of the personal belief that it is impossible to use too much black pepper in this dish
